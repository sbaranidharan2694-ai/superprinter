# SEO growth playbook — Super Printers

**Honest expectation:** Nobody can promise “#1 on Google.” Rankings depend on **competition**, **reviews**, **backlinks**, **content**, **technical SEO**, and **Google Business Profile**. This site’s code now supports strong technical SEO; **your ongoing work** below is what moves you toward the top.

---

## 1. Google Business Profile (highest impact for local printing)

- Claim and complete **Google Business Profile** for Super Printers (address, hours, phone, categories).
- Primary category: **Print shop** (or closest match). Add secondary: **Wedding store**, **Sign shop**, etc. if accurate.
- Post **weekly** (new offers, festival cards, corporate packs).
- Add **photos** weekly: shop front, machines, finished work, team.
- **Reply to every review** (professional, keyword-natural: “Thanks for choosing our Pallavaram printing press…”).

## 2. Reviews (trust + local pack)

- Aim for **steady new Google reviews** (not a burst then silence). Ask happy customers after delivery.
- Never buy fake reviews — Google penalises this.

## 3. Content (rank for long-tail queries)

- Keep publishing **blog** posts that answer real searches, e.g.  
  “Visiting card GSM Chennai”, “Wedding card printing cost Pallavaram”, “GST bill book printing”.
- Each **service page** should have **unique**, helpful copy (you already have slugs — expand where thin).
- Use **Tamil + English** headings where your customers search bilingually.

## 4. Backlinks & citations

- List the business on **Justdial, Sulekha, IndiaMART** (if appropriate), **local Chennai directories**, **wedding vendor lists**.
- Partner with **event planners / corporates** — some will link to you.
- **Same NAP** everywhere: name, address, phone exactly as on the website.

## 5. Technical (already improved in codebase)

- **Structured data:** LocalBusiness, WebSite + Organization, FAQ on homepage, ItemList on `/services`, **Service** schema on wedding/visiting/brochure/bill-book/letterhead landing pages.
- **`llms.txt`** for AI-oriented summaries.
- **Sitemap + robots.txt** — submit sitemap in **Google Search Console**.
- **Core Web Vitals** — keep images optimised; site uses lazy loading and code-splitting.
- When you move to a **custom domain**, update `BUSINESS.siteUrl` in `src/data/business.ts`, `index.html` canonicals/OG URLs, `public/sitemap.xml`, and `public/robots.txt` in one go.

## 6. Track progress

- **Google Search Console:** impressions, clicks, queries.
- **GA4** (when you add a real measurement ID): traffic to `/services/*`, `/blog/*`, `/products`.

## 7. AI search (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Copilot, etc.)

- **`/llms.txt`** and **`/.well-known/llms.txt`** summarise your business for AI crawlers — update when services or contact info changes.
- **`robots.txt`** allows major AI bots — adjust only if you intentionally want to block them.
- **`/llm.html`** is a plain HTML facts page for simple crawlers.
- **Accurate NAP** everywhere helps AI cite you correctly. **#1 in AI answers is not guaranteed.**

## 8. What to avoid

- Keyword stuffing in titles (looks spammy).
- Duplicate content across pages.
- Claiming “#1” or guarantees in meta tags — focus on **accurate, helpful** descriptions.

---

**Summary:** Code gives you a **solid base**. **#1** requires **months** of **reviews + content + GBP + links** in a competitive city like Chennai. Prioritise **Google Business Profile + reviews + local content** first.
